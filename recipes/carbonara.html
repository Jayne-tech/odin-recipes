Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

Spaghetti Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish known for its simplicity and rich flavors. It typically consist of al dente spaghetti noodles tossed with a creamy sauce made from eggs, grated Pecorino Romano or Parmesan cheese, and crispy pancetta or guanciale (cured pork jowl). The eggs and cheese create a velvety coating that clings to the pasta, giving it a luxurious, slightly cheesy taste. The pancetta or guanciale adds a salty and savory element, enhancing the overall profile of the dish.

Ingredients

  • 400g of spaghetti pasta
  • 150g of pancetta or bacon
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup of grated parmesan cheese
  • 3 cloves minced garlic
  • Freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish

Steps

  1. Cook spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a pan, cook the pancetta and bacon until cripsy. Remove from heat and set aside.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, grated Parmesan cheese, and a pinch of black pepper.
  4. In a separate pan, saute minced garlic in olive oil until golden.
  5. Add cooked spaghetti to the pan and toss to continue with the garlic and oil.
  6. Remove from heat and quickly mix in the egg and cheese mixture until creamy.
  7. Stir in the cooked pancetta or bacon. Add salt and more black pepper to taste.
  8. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley and additional Parmesan before serving.
